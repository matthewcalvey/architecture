## Issues That STILL PERTAIN

### 1. Sunshine Skyway Bridge Air-Draft Limitation — NOT ADDRESSED
**Severity: Moderate**

The website never mentions the ~180-foot air-draft clearance imposed by the Sunshine Skyway Bridge, which limits the size of cruise ships that can reach Port Tampa Bay. Vessels that fit under the bridge generally carry 2,000–2,500 passengers.

This matters because:
- The design day of 3,000 pax and growth scenario of 4,500–5,000 pax implies ships larger than what can currently transit the Skyway
- The SSA Marine proposal existed *specifically* to circumvent this limitation (the website discusses SSA Marine but never explains *why* it was proposed)
- Any evaluator familiar with Tampa's port will know this constraint

**Recommendation:** Add a brief note in Context (02) or Scalability (05.E) acknowledging the Skyway constraint and how T-5's capacity targets relate to it (e.g., multiple simultaneous homeported vessels, future bridge considerations, or the channel deepening project addressing draft but not air draft). Even one sentence prevents evaluators from thinking the team is unaware.

### 2. "Five Critical Moves" vs. "Three Critical Design Moves" — Header Mismatch
**Severity: Minor**

- Section 05 header: `<h2>Five Critical Moves</h2>`
- Subsection 05.0: `Three Critical Design Moves`
- Then five more lettered ideas (A–E) follow

The math doesn't add up at a glance. The "Five" likely refers to ideas A–E, while 05.0 covers three core structural moves separately, making seven total items.

**Recommendation:** Change the `<h2>` to "Design Moves & Opportunities" or "Three Moves + Five Ideas" or similar.

### 3. "$1.2 billion Master Plan Vision 2030" — Misleading Attribution
**Severity: Moderate**

Line 605: *"Port Tampa Bay's Channelside district, within the **$1.2 billion Master Plan Vision 2030** redevelopment envelope."*

- The original Vision 2030 (December 2016) was ~$148.5M in port investments
- The $1.2B figure refers to the **channel deepening project** (Army Corps of Engineers partnership)
- December 2025 reports cite an even higher $1.5B estimate for channel deepening

An evaluator who knows PTB's history may flag this conflation.

**Recommendation:** Reword to something like "PTB's Master Plan Vision 2030 and multi-billion-dollar capital program including the $1.2B channel deepening initiative." Or simply say "within the Vision 2030 redevelopment envelope" without the dollar figure.

### 4. Ybor Harbor: "~5,000 residential units" — Minor Inaccuracy
**Severity: Low**

The approved plan specifies **4,750 units** (with 10% affordable). The "~5,000" is a ~5% overstatement. Since this is third-party data an evaluator could verify, precision is preferable.

**Recommendation:** Change to "4,750 residential units."

### 5. CBP/FIS Convertibility — Aggressive Classification (from original review)
**Severity: Moderate (strategic)**

Space B.02 (CBP/FIS, 6,000 SF) is marked as **"Full Convert"** to pickleball/fitness. Federal inspection spaces are governed by CBP's Cruise Terminal Design Standards, which typically require secure storage for inspection equipment, interview rooms maintaining security features, and APHIS areas with specialized infrastructure.

The website correctly marks E.02 (CBP Office Suite) as "Fixed," but classifying the entire FIS primary hall as "Full Convert" is aggressive. An evaluator with CBP coordination experience may question this.

**Recommendation:** Reclassify B.02 to "Partial" or add a note: "Subject to CBP concurrence on demountable partition strategy."

### 6. No Cost Framework (from original review)
**Severity: Low-Moderate**

Public reporting cites ~$80M for the terminal. Comparable terminals on the website: CT3 ($163M/188K SF = ~$867/SF), Terminal A ($250M/170K SF = ~$1,471/SF). The website presents cost data for comparables but doesn't contextualize what this means for T-5.

**Recommendation:** This may not be needed for an RFQ (qualifications-based, not price-based), but adding a brief cost context note using the comparable data already on the site would demonstrate budget awareness. The first-year $3M funding note in Section 00.1 is a good start.
